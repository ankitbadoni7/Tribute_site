William Shakespeare

1564-1616

Birth & Early Life:

William Shakespeare was born on April 23, 1564, in Stratford-upon-Avon, England, to a glove-maker named John Shakespeare and a woman named Mary Arden. He has an enigmatic early life, but likely went to the local grammar school, where he learned Latin and classical literature.

Rise to Fame:

By the late 1580s or early 1590s Shakespeare had settled in London, where he worked as an actor and a playwright. He was by 1594 a principal member of The Lord Chamberlain's Men, thereafter known as The King's Men, a famous theatre company. His plays were staged at the legendary Globe Theatre.

Literary Legacy:

Shakespeare wrote 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and two narrative poems. His works are divided into:

Dramas - Hamlet, Macbeth, Othello, King Lear, Romeo and Juliet Comedies - A Midsummer Night's Dream, Twelfth Night, Much Ado About Nothing Histories - Richard III, Henry V, Henry IV

His words, that combine an unparalleled lyrical genius with a profound understanding of humanity, still speak to us all across the globe today.

Famous Quotes:

* “To be, or not to be, that is the question.” – Hamlet * “All the world’s a stage, and all the men and women merely players.” – As You Like It * “Some are born great, some achieve greatness, and some have greatness thrust upon them.” – Twelfth Night

Later Years & Death:

Shakespeare retired to Stratford-upon-Avon around 1610 and lived in peace there until he died on April 23, 1616 — his 52nd birthday.

Enduring Influence:

His works have stood the test of time and have influenced literature, theatre, and even everyday language. His legacy, known as the "Bard of Avon," remains unparalleled, and his words live on to inspire generations.
